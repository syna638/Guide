Project Overview

Understanding what Smart Civic Connect does and the problems it solves

What is Smart Civic Connect?

Imagine you're walking in your neighborhood and you see a big pothole in the road, or a broken streetlight that's been out for weeks. What do you do? In most cities, you might:

  • Call a government helpline (and wait on hold forever)
  • Visit a government office (taking hours out of your day)
  • Send an email (and hope someone reads it)
  • Do nothing (because it's too much hassle)

Smart Civic Connect solves this problem!

It's a web application that lets citizens report civic issues directly from their phone or computer. You take a photo, describe the problem, and submit it. The government officials receive your complaint, track its progress, and resolve it ‚Äì all through the same platform.

üìò Think of it like...

Smart Civic Connect is like an online complaint box combined with a package tracking system. You submit a complaint, and you can track its status from "Pending" to "In Progress" to "Resolved" ‚Äì just like tracking a delivery!

How It Works: User Journey

Citizen's Journey

Sign Up / Sign In

Create an account using email, Google, or other methods. This takes less than a minute.

Report an Issue

Click "New Complaint," take a photo, select the category, describe the problem, and mark the location.

Submit

Hit submit and your complaint goes directly to the relevant department.

Track Progress

Check your complaints list anytime to see the current status and any admin responses.

Get Notified

Receive notifications when your complaint is acknowledged, being worked on, or resolved.

Administrator's Journey

Secure Login

Log in using admin credentials (separate from citizen accounts for security).

View Dashboard

See all pending complaints, filter by category or status, and prioritize urgent issues.

Update Status

Change complaint status from "Pending" to "In Progress" when work begins.

Resolve & Document

Mark as "Resolved" and add notes or photos showing the completed work.
